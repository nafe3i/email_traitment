   ├─ Enregistrement des utilisateurs

   ├─ Traçage de tous les emails traités
   ├─ Champs: sender, subject, body, response, status, cost
   └─ Capacity: Millions of email records

   ├─ Documents pour RAG/embeddings
   ├─ Champs: title, content, category, tags, embedding_model
   └─ Capacity: Thousands of documents

4. api_tokens (Accès API)
   ├─ Tokens pour accès programmatique
   ├─ Champs: token_hash, scopes, expires_at
   └─ Capacity: Unlimited tokens per user

5. audit_logs (Logs de sécurité)
   ├─ Événements sensibles (login, password change, etc)
   ├─ Champs: action, user_id, ip_address, status
   └─ Capacity: Billions of audit records

💡 KEY INSIGHTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Why Phase 1 uses in-memory storage:
  ✅ Simple to implement & understand
  ✅ Good for testing authentication logic
  ✅ No external dependencies needed
  ✅ Fast iteration during development
  ⚠️  Not suitable for production

Why Phase 2 switches to PostgreSQL:
  ✅ Persistent storage (data survives restarts)
  ✅ Scalable to millions of users
  ✅ ACID compliance (reliable)
  ✅ Audit logging built-in
  ✅ Encryption capabilities
  ✅ Backup & recovery
  ✅ Production-ready
